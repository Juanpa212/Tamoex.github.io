Intel vs AMD

When it comes to variety AMD is better than Intel. With far more efficent processors and not to mention cheaper, AMD is the better option if you are on a budget. However, Intel is the clear winner when it comes to raw power.

Nvidia vs AMD

When it comes to graphics cards there are two main competitors, Nvidia and AMD. Nvidia is the more popular of the two and is the one that is used in most gaming pcs. AMD is the less popular of the two and is the one that is used in most prebuilt pcs. Both are good options, but Nvidia is the better option if you are a gamer.

Solid State Drive vs Hard Drive

When it comes to read and write speeds the SSD takes the cake. However, SSDs are more expensive than hard drives. A hard drive is a good option if you are on a budget, but if you have the money to spend, an SSD is the way to go. Note: There are external options that can be purchased in local retailers. These options are like pen drives but on steroids. They connect via USB and can be used to store files. though again the tend to be more expensive than the ladder.

Atx vs MicroAtx

These are the two most common types of motherboards. The difference between the two is the size. The Atx is the bigger of the two and the MicroAtx is the smaller of the two. The Atx is the more common of the two and is the one that is used in most gaming pcs. The MicroAtx is the smaller of the two and is used in smaller pcs. The MicroAtx is the one that is used in most prebuilt pcs.

Air Cooling vs Water Cooling vs Custom Loop

Air cooling is the cheaper option most of the time and when running lower power componets its a good option. However, when running higher power components, you would need to swap to a water cooling system or custom loop. These systems are more expensive, but they are more efficent at cooling higher power components. Custom loops are a more advance option that can be scary at first but it delivers the best results and by far the best looks.

Add-ons

These are the components that are not crucial to the performance of your pc, but they are nice to have. A dvd drive is a good option if you have a lot of dvd discs. A sound card is a good option if you are an audiophile. A wifi card is a good option if you do not have an ethernet cable. RGB lights are a good option if you want your pc to look cool.
