Processor vs Graphics

Each component is crucial to the performance of your pc. So carefully reseach each component and make sure it suits your need. For example if you are a gamer you will want to invest more in your graphics card than your processor, because a graphics card is the key to achieving the high level frames that make the gaming experience feel smooth. If you are a video editor you will want to invest more in your processor than your Graphics card, because a processor is the key to achieving the high level rendering that makes a video editing workflow feel more fluid.

How much Ram do i need?

Normally the more amount of ram in a system, the better you system will be able to allocate resources to different tasks. However, if you are a gamer you will not need more than 16gb of ram. If you are a video editor you will want to invest in 32gb of ram or more. Because of the amount of files you are constantly working with, and with that comes storage.

Storage

The amount of storage you need depends on the amount of files you are working with. If you are a gamer you will want to invest in a 1tb hard drive, because games are getting bigger and bigger. If you are a video editor you will want to invest in a 2tb hard drive, because video files are huge.

Motherboard and Power Supply

These two are the bread and butter of the computer they are the foundation of the system. The motherboard is the component that connects all the other components together. The power supply is the component that provides power to all the other components. When it comes to these two components you will want to invest in quality. If you have a bad motherboard or power supply, you will have a bad time.

Case and Cooling

These components offer the most amout of variety. There are many different types of cases and cooling systems that you can chose from, normally a processor comes with a fan but if you invest in a better cooling system it will help your system run quieter and cooler
